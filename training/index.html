Instructions
Science

Our brain rewires everyday. By exploiting this neural plasticity, we can achieve long-term structural and functional changes. In other words, we are capable of learning abstract skills based on motor-imagery to extend our output streams, enabling us to manipulate technology just with thought.

When we move our left hand, a particular place in the right sensorymotor cortex is activated in our brain. This is known as lateralization of motor activity. When we think about moving our hand, some of the pathways in the brain are activated as if we were actually performing the physical movement. The mental recitation of a motor movement is called motor imagery.

Information is transferred in the brain through electric potentials. Using electroencephalography (EEG), we can measure such activity. Because of the nature of EEG, there is a bunch of noise and unnecessary information. Thus, we use signal processing to filter unwanted features and extract a certain range of frequencies. These processed signals are then fed to a machine learning algorithm which learns how to classify when a user is thinking left, right, or baseline. Once the algorithm has learned how to differentiate between these three mind-states, it acts as a translator from motor-imagery to commands for any electronic device.
